1. Commitment to GDPR Compliance

This document confirms that Nani Recruitment Ltd agrees to fully uphold the principles and legal obligations of the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) when collecting, processing, storing, or sharing personal data.

  • Lawfulness, Fairness, and Security: All personal and sensitive data is handled lawfully, fairly, and securely.
  • Purpose Limitation: Data is shared only for relevant purposes such as work placements, compliance checks, or legal obligations.
  • Integrity & Confidentiality: Appropriate safeguards (e.g., encryption, access control) are in place to protect all data.
  • Transparency: All data subjects are informed of why their data is collected, who it may be shared with, and their full GDPR rights.
  • Accountability: Any data breaches will be reported and investigated in line with UK GDPR obligations.

2. Lawful Basis for Sharing & Our Role

We only share personal data when we have a lawful basis to do so:

  • Consent: The individual has given clear consent.
  • Contract: It is necessary to fulfill a contract (e.g., job placement).
  • Legal Obligation: It is required to meet a legal obligation (e.g., DBS, Right to Work checks).
  • Legitimate Interest: It is based on a legitimate interest with minimal risk to individual rights.

Our Role:

  • Data Controller: For our own internal operations and processes.
  • Data Processor: When handling data on behalf of our clients.

3. Data Sharing Practices

We adhere to strict protocols when sharing personal data with third parties:

  • Data Minimization: Only relevant and minimal data is shared.
  • Signed Agreements: Data Sharing Agreements (DSAs) and Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) are signed with all third parties.
  • Enhanced Protection: Special category data (e.g., health, DBS records) is shared with enhanced protection measures.

4. Security Safeguards

We implement robust security measures to protect your personal information:

  • Encryption: All data is transmitted via encrypted email or secure cloud platforms.
  • Access Control: Staff access is strictly role-based.
  • Secure Storage: All files are password-protected and stored securely.
  • Internal Audits: We conduct regular audits to ensure compliance.

6. Individual Rights under GDPR

We uphold all your rights as a data subject, including:

  • Right to be informed: You have the right to know how your data is being used.
  • Right of access: You can request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification: You can request that inaccurate or incomplete data be corrected.
  • Right to erasure: You can request the deletion of your data (subject to legal retention requirements).
  • Right to restrict processing: You can ask us to limit the way we use your data.
  • Right to data portability: You can request your data in a format that allows it to be transferred to another organisation.
  • Right to object: You can object to the processing of your data in certain circumstances.
  • Rights related to automated profiling: You have rights regarding automated decision-making.

7. Subject Access Request (SAR) Process

To request your personal data, please follow this process:

  • How to request: Email your request to info@nanirecruitment.com.
  • Response Time: We will respond to your request within 30 calendar days.
  • Verification: We may require proof of identity to verify your request.

8. Data Breach Procedure

In the event of a data breach, we follow a strict protocol:

  • Reporting: The breach will be reported to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) within 72 hours.
  • Notification: Affected individuals will be notified if the breach poses a risk to their rights and freedoms.
  • Investigation: The incident will be logged, investigated, and corrected.

9. International Data Transfers

If we share data outside the UK, we ensure adequate safeguards are in place, such as:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs): We use approved legal mechanisms for data transfers.
  • Logging and Review: All international data transfers are logged and reviewed for risk.

10. Oversight & Review

This policy is overseen by our Data Protection Officer and is subject to regular review:

  • Policy Review: Annually, when regulations change, or when business operations change.
